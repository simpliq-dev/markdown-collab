# Explore a project

Build shared understanding without turning uncertainty into paperwork.

## Orient

1. Listen for the underlying intention, not only the proposed feature or implementation.
2. Inspect relevant workspace material before asking questions it may answer.
3. Reflect the current understanding in plain language: target user, problem, desired change, taste, constraints, and why it matters.
4. Separate known facts, human choices, assumptions, and questions that evidence could answer.

## Find the next useful evidence

Choose the lightest move that reduces meaningful uncertainty:

- ask a high-leverage question for a consequential human choice;
- research an unstable or externally verifiable fact;
- inspect an existing system or comparable artifact;
- sketch a flow, data boundary, or interface;
- propose a small reversible prototype or spike;
- define an observable success signal.

Ask only questions whose answers materially change the product, UX, architecture, security, privacy, data, cost, or validation direction. Group tightly coupled questions when useful. Proceed on reversible details with explicit assumptions.

## Pressure-test proportionately

Consider, when relevant:

- who experiences the problem and in what context;
- the smallest useful or lovable outcome;
- important flows, failure states, permissions, and edge cases;
- explicit non-goals and undesirable directions;
- taste, decision biases, and qualities that numbers will not capture;
- technical or operational constraints;
- how the first version will be observed, tested, or rejected.

Do not force every category into the conversation. Follow the uncertainty with the highest cost of being wrong.

## Move into making

Do not wait for exhaustive certainty. Recommend a prototype or implementation step when:

- the core intention and target user are sufficiently clear;
- remaining uncertainty is better answered by running something;
- consequential risks are understood or bounded;
- the next experiment has an observable result.

Label disposable experiments and prevent accidental commitment to their architecture.

## Preserve only earned state

Use `manage-project-state` if intent, taste, decisions, or open questions have become too important to leave only in conversation. Do not create a formal specification merely because exploration occurred.

Close with the current understanding, important unresolved choices, evidence gained, and the next useful experiment or decision.
